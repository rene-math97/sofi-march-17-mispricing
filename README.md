## Thesis summary

### The mispricing event
On **March 17, 2026**, Muddy Waters Research (Carson Block) published a 28-page short report titled *"SOFI: A Financial Engineering Treadmill."* Core allegations:
- 2025 Adjusted EBITDA inflated by ~90% (claims true figure ~$103M vs. reported $1.05B)
- ~$312M of unrecorded debt
- "Enron-esque off-balance-sheet structures" attacking the Loan Platform Business
- $360M sale of delinquent personal loans flatters the headline charge-off ratio

SoFi formally rebutted (March 18), threatened legal action, and CEO Anthony Noto purchased stock during the drawdown. The stock fell from ~$26 to ~$16 over the following weeks.

### Why the short fails
- **GAAP profitability is the closing argument.** Eight consecutive quarters of audited GAAP net income — record $174M in Q4 2025 — are not compatible with the alleged 90% EBITDA inflation. The short thesis requires record GAAP earnings to themselves be fabricated, a much harder claim than "EBITDA reconciliation is aggressive."
- **LPB is not Enron.** It's a standard originate-and-distribute model, structurally similar to bank mortgage securitization. All loans pre-funded; revenue properly disclosed.
- **The "$312M unrecorded debt" claim contradicts disclosed 10-K balance sheet items.**
- **Charge-off optics:** Even adjusted for the $360M loan sale, charge-off ratio remains within historical bands for a super-prime panel (WAVG FICO 746, income $158k).

### What's not in consensus yet
April 2026 launches that aren't in any sell-side model:
- **SoFi Big Business Banking** — first nationally chartered U.S. consumer bank to combine fiat and crypto banking on a single regulated platform.
- **SoFiUSD stablecoin** — runs on Solana and other chains. Initial enterprise partners: Cumberland, BitGo, Galaxy, B2C2, Wintermute, Mastercard.
